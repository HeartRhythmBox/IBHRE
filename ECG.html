☰ Essential ECGs for IBHRE
The Basic
Bradyarrhythmias
(mouse over image to show info)
1st degree AV block. In a severe case (PR>...), symptoms similar to pacemaker syndrome may occur.
Mobitz I or Wenckebach. Pacemaker is indicated if symptomatic.
Complete heart block with slow escape rate; RBBB morphology.
intermittent complete heart block. 2Ps without any QRS.
Bifascicular block with 1st degree AV block. Pacemaker is indicated (IIa) in a patient with unexplained syncope.
Sinus pause
Sinoatrial exit block type II.

Tachyarrhythmias
(mouse over image to show info)
Atrial Fibrillation: irregularly irregular rhythm. Noted with fibrillatory waves in V1.
Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT): Narrow complex tachycardia with no visible P wave.
Polymorphic VT with long QT or Torsade de Pointes.
Idiopathic VT from RV outflow tract. Noted with positive QRS in II, III, and aVF; normal R wave progression in chest leads.
Ischemic VT in a patient with history of inferior wall MI.
❮ ❯
Conditions with risk of sudden cardiac arrest
(mouse over image to show info)
Brugada ECG: J point elevation with coved-ST elevation in V1-V2.
Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy: high voltage in left-sided leads with ST depression and T wave inversion.
Long QT syndrome.
Old MI; Noted with Q waves in inferior and anterior leads.
